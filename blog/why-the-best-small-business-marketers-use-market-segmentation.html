Why the Best Small Business Marketers Use Market Segmentation

Preston · February 21, 2023

As you start to become more successful in your business, you may start to hear the word Market Segmentation. What is market segmentation? If you are a small business, does geographic market segmentation matter? The fact of the day is most effective and efficient small business marketers use market segmentation to get a better understanding of their customers. In today's blog post, I will be writing about how you can create a badass local marketing strategy using market segmentation and behavioral analytics.

What is Market Segmentation?

Market Segmentation is fairly easy to explain but very difficult to implement well. Before you start segmenting your marketing plan, you need to understand Behavioral marketing.

Behavioral marketing is the way companies target audiences based on their interests, behavior, intentions, and other metrics using digital analytics tools like search history, cookies, and other insights.

Customer segmentation is the process of isolating these interests and behaviors to better target your target demographic.

Implementing an effective market segmentation strategy is a bit like being a detective because you need to look at trends, clues, and engagement metrics to better learn how to convert them into sales or customers. When you are practicing market segmentation correctly, you will soar.

A study from HubSpot found that CTRs that utilize behavioral marketing or personalization can increase a 760% increase in revenue! So without any further ado, let's talk about market segmentation and how to segment your customers for better revenue.

The Best Tools for Tracking Market Segments and Identifying Comprehensive Behavioral Targeting

Tracking your different marketing segments to know how to target them better starts with good analytics. Many people have heard of Google Analytics but just because it's free doesn't mean it's the best.

For general converting website visitors into leads, check out Google Analytics

Google Analytics does its job well and is increasingly becoming the most popular tool out there for understanding where people come from and how to market to them better. But depending on your industry, google analytics might not be the best tool for you.

Facebook insights are the marketing and analytics tools that you can use on the most popular social media site on the internet (unless you consider googling a social media source). If you know you already have a lot of traffic from Facebook, this might be the best analytics tool to identify your different market segments.

For a 3d and visual alternative to Google Analytics, use Smartlook Analytics.

I love google analytics, but it can be a bit 2d for my taste. I am a very visual person and it can be hard for me to put together a full picture without 3d perspectives on tactile data.

That's where Big Red Jelly uses Smartlook. On our Support and Strategy team, we work with people every day using smart look insights to help improve their marketing segmentation over time. We actually really love the smart look and no, that's not a sponsored link.
